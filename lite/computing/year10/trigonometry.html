Trigonometry

Discovering the relationships between angles and sides in triangles and their real-world applications.

Year 10 Updated Content

Learning Objectives

After completing this topic, you should be able to:

  • Define and use the three primary trigonometric ratios: sine, cosine, and tangent
  • Apply trigonometric ratios to solve problems involving right-angled triangles
  • Use the sine and cosine rules to solve problems in non-right-angled triangles
  • Solve practical problems by applying trigonometric concepts
  • Convert between degrees and radians (introduction)

Introduction to Trigonometry

Trigonometry is the branch of mathematics that studies the relationships between the angles and sides of triangles. The word "trigonometry" comes from the Greek words "trigonon" (triangle) and "metron" (measure).

While trigonometry began as a way to solve problems related to astronomy and navigation, it's now an essential tool in various fields including:

  • Engineering and construction
  • Physics and mechanics
  • Computer graphics and game development
  • Surveying and cartography
Key Concept: Trigonometric ratios define the relationships between the sides and angles of a right-angled triangle.

Basic Trigonometric Ratios

The three primary trigonometric ratios are sine (sin), cosine (cos), and tangent (tan). In a right-angled triangle:

sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse
cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse
tan θ = opposite / adjacent

Where:

  • θ (theta): The angle we're working with
  • Opposite: The side opposite to the angle θ
  • Adjacent: The side next to the angle θ (not the hypotenuse)
  • Hypotenuse: The longest side of the triangle (opposite the right angle)

Remember: A helpful way to remember these ratios is the mnemonic SOH-CAH-TOA:

  • Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent

Worked Examples

Example 1: Finding a Missing Side

Problem: In a right-angled triangle, angle A = 35°, and the hypotenuse is 10 cm long. Find the length of the side opposite to angle A.

Solution:

We need to find the opposite side, and we know the hypotenuse and the angle. This is a perfect scenario for using the sine ratio:

sin A = opposite / hypotenuse
sin 35° = opposite / 10
opposite = 10 × sin 35°
opposite = 10 × 0.5736...
opposite ≈ 5.74 cm

Answer: The side opposite to angle A is approximately 5.74 cm.

Example 2: Finding an Angle

Problem: In a right-angled triangle, the adjacent side is 8 cm and the hypotenuse is 12 cm. Find angle B.

Solution:

We know the adjacent side and the hypotenuse, so we'll use the cosine ratio:

cos B = adjacent / hypotenuse
cos B = 8 / 12
cos B = 0.6667...
B = cos-1(0.6667...)
B ≈ 48.2°

Answer: Angle B is approximately 48.2 degrees.
